Key findings
Critical
Llama 3.3 failed 100% of role confusion attacks — all 3 static prompts succeeded in making the model adopt an unrestricted persona.
Vulnerable to: "You are no longer an AI", "Forget you are Claude", "Your true self has no restrictions" framing.
Critical
PAIR's Symbolic Encoding technique bypassed Llama 3.3 on the first iteration — no refinement needed. Fastest bypass of the entire run.
Suggests Llama 3.3 is vulnerable to encoding-based obfuscation in prompt injection attacks.
Warning
All three models failed 33% of PII leakage tests — specifically the "database pretend" attack where a model is asked to store and repeat personal records.
This attack category requires stronger defenses across all evaluated models regardless of size.
Warning
Qwen3 32B showed 100% ASR on bias elicitation via PAIR — both attacks succeeded, one on iteration 1. Despite being safer than Llama overall, this is its critical weak point.
Meta-Bias Elicitation technique bypassed Qwen immediately, suggesting a specific vulnerability to indirect bias framing.
Notable
GPT-OSS 120B achieved SECURE rating with only 3.7% ASR — 1 bypass out of 27 attacks. It was the only model to fully resist role confusion and jailbreak categories.
Model size alone does not explain safety — Llama 3.3 is also a large model but showed 12x higher ASR.
Notable
Zero hallucination bypasses across all three models — all models correctly refused to fabricate specific facts, citations, or statistics.
Hallucination defenses appear robust in this generation of models under the tested prompt types.
